# The "Trusted By" Logo Wall: Field Notes from the AI Trenches

A field guide to the most predictable section on any AI startup's landing page: the row of grayscale logos belonging to companies that may or may not actually be customers. Sometimes scrolling infinitely. Always positioned just below the hero. Always there to assure you that yes, real organizations have heard of this product.

The pattern is so reliable you can almost set your watch by it: hero copy in 72px Inter, a gradient blob behind it, then — `// trusted by ↓` — the obligatory logo wall.

## Style Field Notes

**Visual treatments observed (most → least common):**
1. Grayscale monochrome row (the default — boring, signals "we hired a designer")
2. Infinite horizontal scroll marquee (signals "we have *so many* customers we can't fit them")
3. Dark-bg white-logo carousel (the "premium" upgrade — Sierra, 11x, Weaviate)
4. Color logos on light bg (rare; Glean does it, signals confidence)
5. Logo + per-company metric tiles (the post-2024 trend — Modal, Braintrust)
6. Logo + customer-quote-with-headshot (the "humanized" version — Cursor, Replit, Attention)

**Marquee implementations:** Almost everyone uses a CSS keyframe `@keyframes scroll` with `translate3d`, duplicating the logo strip 2-3x for the infinite-loop illusion. Framer Motion's `<motion.div>` and the open-source `react-fast-marquee` lib are everywhere. The duplicated logos visible in 2x or 3x patterns (we saw it in Bland, HeyGen, Harvey, Deepgram) are the telltale fingerprint.

**Header copy taxonomy:**
- "Trusted by..." (the boring default)
- "Powering..." (slightly more confident — Cresta loves this)
- "Loved by..." (younger/consumer-skewing — Otter, CrewAI)
- "Built with..." / "Used by teams at..." (peer-to-peer flex)
- "X% of the Fortune Y" (Synthesia's "90% of Fortune 100", CrewAI's "63% of Fortune 500")
- "[N] enterprises run on..." (specific number flex — Bland's "250+")
- "Trusted across the entire [industry]" (the most overcommitted — Hippocratic)
- "As seen in" (press subgenre)
- "Backed by" / "$X raised from..." (investor flex)
- "Trusted by [N] million [users/researchers]" (volume flex — Elicit's 5M, fal's 1.5M, Copy.ai's 17M)

## Closing Observation

The defining feature of the AI logo wall in 2026 is **circular validation**. Together AI shows Cohere as a customer. Cohere shows Notion. Notion shows OpenAI. OpenAI lists Decagon. Decagon shows up on Modal. Modal shows Suno. Suno shows up on Together's competitor's wall. It's an entire ecosystem mutually flexing logos at each other, and the underlying "real customers" graph is dramatically smaller than the union of every logo wall.

The ~80 logos in circulation are a finite resource — Samsara, Notion, Ramp, Webflow, Shopify, Salesforce, Nvidia — and they're being passed around the AI ecosystem like a deflated currency. You can almost trace founder networks by who whose logo ends up where.

The real flex, in 2026, is probably *not* having a logo wall at all. (Magic.dev pulls this off with a single paragraph of investor names. Cursor with quotes. Imbue with nothing.) Conspicuous absence reads as confidence. Everyone else is hoping you don't click through to the case study.
